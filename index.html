The 6 jar saving method

The single biggest difference between financial success and financial failure is how well you manage your money. Managing your money allos you to eventually create financial freedom so that you never have to work again.



T. Harv Eker, author of the New York Times best-selling book, “Secrets of the Millionair Mind”, has the simplest, most effective money management system - the JARS Money Management System - that you can start using to manage your money today.



To use this system, simply set up 6 bank accounts or use actual jars and each time you receive income, divide your money up in the jars using the guidelines below.



Remember, to master money, you must manage money.

Necessities

In order to have healthy finances, this indicator must be less or equal to the ideal percentage shown in the input. Necessities are related with rent, food (groceries), home bills, transportations, everythings that is indispensable.

Play

In order to have healthy finances, this indicator must be less or equal to the ideal percentage shown in the input. Play is related with spoiling yourself, buying things, hanging out, friendship, love, family, whatever spend you have that is not a necessity.

Financial freedom

In order to have healthy finances, this indicator must be greater or equal to the ideal percentage shown in the input. Financial freedom consists in every save you have addressed to increase your income and your savings.

Long term savings for spending

In order to have healthy finances, this indicator must be greater or equal to the ideal percentage shown in the input. Long term savings is related to all the savings that you have to cover any emergency, any job loss, any circumstance, without using your main income.

Education

This indicator must be greater or equal to the ideal percentage shown in the input. One of the main investments a person must do to get financial freedom is to invest in education. This indicator shows how much you are saving in education.

Give

This indicator is related with your giving, to have a good and healthy finances thinking, giving is a good start because it helps you detach from money.
